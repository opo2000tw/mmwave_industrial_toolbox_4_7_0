Hardware Setup of IWRXXXXBOOST for Functional Mode =========== This guide is intended for users of an **IWRXXXXBOOST evaluation modules** (EVM). The guide will walk through setting up the boards into **Functional Mode**. In functional mode, the EVM boots autonomously from flash and starts running the bin image. <img src="images/iwrxxxxboost/iwrxxxxboost_evm.png" width="700"/> # 0. Requirements ### Required and Supported mmWave Evaluation Modules (EVM) Quantity | Item ------------------|----------------- 1 | IWRXXXXBOOST Board: [IWR1443BOOST](http://www.ti.com/tool/IWR1443BOOST), [IWR1642BOOST](http://www.ti.com/tool/IWR1642BOOST), or [IWR1843BOOST](http://www.ti.com/tool/IWR1843BOOST) ### Additional Requirements Quantity | Item | Details ---------|--------------------------|----------------- 1 | Micro USB Cable | Provided with the IWRXXXXBOOST board 1 | Power Supply | 5V, 3A with 2.1-mm barrel jack (center positive). The power supply can be wall adapter style or a battery pack with a USB to barrel jack cable. {{b Step 1 may be skipped if the hardware setup has not change since following the guide for [Hardware Setup of IWRXXXXBOOST for Flashing Mode](../hardware_setup/hw_setup_boost_evm_for_flashing.html) }} # 1. Connect micro-USB and Power * Connect the micro-USB to the EVM at the XDS110 and then to the PC. * Connect the 5V/3A power supply to the power source and then to the EVM. LEDs on the carrier board should turn on. As an example, the image below shows the connection points on an IWR1642BOOST. The relative location of connection points are similar for all IWRXXXXBOOST. **Note**: For the IWR1843BOOST the barrel jack connector for power is on the bottom side of the board. <img src="images/iwrxxxxboost/iwrxxxxboost_connect_cable.png" width="400"/> # 2. Set SOP Mode for Functional Mode Enable functional mode by setting SOP2:0 to 001 as shown below. Depending on the EVM, the board may have jumpers or switches to set the SOP Mode - refer to the image corresponding to your EVM type. <img src="images/iwrxxxxboost/iwrxxxxboost_sop_mode_functional.png" width="800"/> # 3. Toggle NRST switch To ensure that the correct SOP mode is latched once power has been applied, power cycle by pressing and releasing the NRST switch identified below. The yellow DS4 LED below should toggle OFF and ON when the switch is depressed then released. <img src="images/iwrxxxxboost/iwrxxxxboost_nrst.png" width="300"/> [[y! Toggling NRST After any SOP mode change, NRST should be always be toggled to ensure a reliable boot-up state. ]] <a name="physical_setup"></a> Next Step =========== Now that the hardware has been setup for **Functional Mode**, the demo can be launched. Refer to the demo user's guide for steps to launch the demo and appropriate visualizer.
